âœ¨ Importance of Movement in Facilitating Creativity
Motion is an essential activity that leads to creativity among the little children. It manages to give them an opportunity to understand the way their bodies operate, express themselves, and communicate with their environment creatively. Dance, stretching, jumping and similar physical exercises help children to be confident and communicate. Movement promotes open play and ability to explore. By moving creatively children are able to learn how to interpret music, to narrate their own stories with their movement and also get to solve problems through body movement. Integrating the element of movement into the daily classroom experience, teachers make children more active, conscious, and ready to discover something new (2025).

ðŸ§  Creativity Theories and Perspectives
Creative movement assists children to understand the world around them by use of their bodies. Concept of children learning through action helps to provide movement to help children develop thinking skills. Children that pretend to be animals, or act out a story through gestures are combining imagination and action. Motivating this type of physical games is to assist children to devote themselves expressively without using words. It is as well a boost in brain development associating thought and movement. The creative movement enables children to find out their emotions, implement social networks and practice self-control in an amusing, interactive manner (2025b).

ðŸ§° Resources, Materials, and Digital Tools
To engender movement, the teachers may offer ribbons, scarves, hoops, beanbags and soft floor mats. Instruments and music players are useful in establishing a rhythm and encouraging the free powerful responses. Outdoors and paths that can be used as obstacles provide children with a chance to climb, to run, to crawl and to have a balance. Even indoors, children can take part in novel movements through simple dance videos or games in motion shown on the screens. Delivery methods, such as interactive whiteboards or tablets, can structure children to practise imagination festival yoga or dance. These settings and wide variety of materials allow children to have the freedom to move which will feel natural to them, expressive, and fun.
